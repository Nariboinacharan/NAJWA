About us

VISION

Role of making people's life better. Meet the needs of asylum seekers and refugees, and enhance the quality of their life, reduce the like hood that a decimation will happen. Get it right for every asylum seeker or refugees’ children, using a child centred approach for new Scots’ children to become successful learners, confident individuals..,effective contributors, and responsible citizens. Ensure Asylum Seekers and Refugees are included by A person centred approach to include them on the support they need and be in control of their own support. Advice and information Working collaboratively with other organisations to ensure the needs of the most vulnerable are met and develop other supporting points. Enhance the participation of Asylum Seekers and Refugees in volunteering and productivity.

MISSION

Welcoming asylum seekers and refugees. Diverse community where new Scots feel valued, respected, equality, dignity and can be person cantered while making their own choice on the support they are receiving. A community where asylum seekers and refugees know their right and be informed and advised on different prospects related to Health and Social care. Maintain their wellbeing, ending loneliness and reduce negative thoughts, socialising. Reaching the maximum number of asylum seekers and refugees who are not yet reached due to language barrier. Giving a change to asylum seekers and refugees to be leaders in the community by changing what is wrong to what is strong on them.

AUDIENCE

Asylum seekers, Refugees: Women, Children, men Carers and Health workers asylum seekers and refugees. BAME British Arab and African Minor Ethnicity Asylum Seekers and refugees. Disabled asylum seekers and refugees. Unpaid carers asylum seekers and refugees. Elderlies asylum seekers and refugees. --

BIO_GRAPHY

Ensuring the sustainability of vulnerable support, and the continuity of delivering projects in safe and regulated process, the founder is and International Certified Senior Health and Social Care worker and charity projects worker that served the community for 24 years, with high standards of care, finance, project development, training, working in schools counselling for children, families and employees, participated in different charity projects in schools, working in care at home care to adult and children patients having Health conditions difficulties. Worked as a school counsellor and did several charity projects within and outside Scotland. Having Strong collaboration with charity organisations in Glasgow knowing what they are offering and linking the vulnerable groups with them to get the necessary support,
